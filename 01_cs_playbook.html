1Operating Philosophy

Before tactics, the principle. This is what separates AI-assisted support from AI-replaced support — and why clients trust the first one.

"AI drafts. Humans review. Customers receive a response that feels personal because a human chose every word — even if a machine suggested the first version."
— Operating principle for every ticket I handle

2Tiered Support Framework

A 3-tier system where AI accelerates volume work, human judgment owns nuance, and engineering only sees what truly needs them. Each tier has clear ownership, response targets, and AI involvement.

TIER 1 · AI-ASSISTED

Routine inquiries

Target: 30-45 sec response · 90%+ same-day resolve
  • Password resets & account access
  • Billing questions & invoice resends
  • Feature discovery & "how do I..." questions
  • Status of known issues
  • Plan changes & basic settings
AI role: Drafts response from template library. Human personalizes name, context, sentiment. Always reviewed before send.
TIER 2 · HUMAN-CENTRIC

Complex / nuanced cases

Target: 90-120 sec response · <24h resolve
  • Bug reports with reproduction steps needed
  • Integration issues (API, webhooks, third-party)
  • Billing disputes & refund requests
  • Multi-step technical troubleshooting
  • Account ownership / data export requests
AI role: Suggests diagnostic steps and surfaces similar past tickets. Human owns judgment on resolution path.
TIER 3 · ENGINEERING

Real bugs & outages

Target: 4h triage · SLA per severity
  • Confirmed product bugs requiring code fix
  • Data integrity issues
  • Security incidents
  • Performance / scaling failures
  • Cross-system integration breakages
AI role: Helps draft structured bug report (steps, expected vs. actual, environment, logs). Human owns engineering handoff and customer communication.

6Quality Control at Volume

How I keep CSAT above 90% even when handling 130+ tickets a day. Volume without quality is just noise — these are the guardrails.

  • ✓
    Every AI draft reviewed before send. No auto-send, no exceptions. Even on the simplest tier 1 tickets, the response gets a human read for tone, context, and accuracy.
  • ✓
    Personalization layer mandatory. AI gives the structure, I add the customer's name, the specific detail they mentioned, and the tonal cue (formal/casual/empathetic depending on their message).
  • ✓
    Weekly CSAT audit. Pull all 1-3 star ratings, read the customer's full conversation, identify what the response missed. Update templates and KB based on findings.
  • ✓
    "Sounds like a robot" filter. If the AI draft starts with "Thank you for reaching out!" or any other generic AI-tell, I rewrite the opening. Real humans don't open emails like that.
  • ✓
    Escalation completeness check. Before any handoff to engineering: repro steps clear, environment specified, customer impact documented, workaround tested. No "FYI broken" tickets.
  • ✓
    Voice consistency across EN/ES. Same brand tone in both languages. No machine-translated Spanish — I write the Spanish version with native judgment, not a Google Translate output.
